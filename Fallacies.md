# Fallacy of False Dichotomy
This occurs when one mistakenly bases arguments on a premise (either stated or unstated) that only two choices are available when a wider range of alternatives exist. This fallacy has many names including black-and-white thinking, false choice, false dilemma and the great either/or.

# Strawman Fallacy (ignoratio elenchi)
Occurs when an argument is directed against a proposition that is presented as though it were the true position of one's opponents, although it is not. By distorting the opponents' argument, premises that do not bear on the real issue are made to seem relevant. The "straw man" is demolished, the opponent's argument ridiculed, but the real controversy is not addressed.

# Ad hominenm
Occurs when a person directs their argument against an individual rather than against the position they hold. It is sometimes referred to as "posoning the well"
