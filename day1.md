# Units
1. Relativism & Social Contract - ethics are determined by the person or the society (relative)
2. Consequentialism - based on the consequences not the act (the end justifies the means)
3. Deontology - right and wrong is based on the act itself not the consequences. Consequences are irrelevant
4. Virtue Ethics - both the act and the consequences are important

# Fundamental Principles
- Who is affected? Who are the stakeholders?
- Are you treating other humans with respect?
- How would the public view your decision? (New York Times test)
- How will the least empowered be impacted?
- How would your actions be judged by the ideal Software Engineer?
